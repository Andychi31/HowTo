How to Throw a Curveball

What is a curveball?

The curveball is a very usual pitch in baseball that a pitcher can throw to get the batter to chase and strike him out. It is a pitch that breaks to right for left-handed pitchers, and breaks to the left for right-handed pitchers. It has been used for centuries of baseball, and still used by almost every pitcher today. Today, I am going to teach you how to throw the curveball. However, it is not easy The best time to throw a curveball, is on an 0-2 count..

How to Throw the Curveball

Gripping the Curveball

There is a specific way to grip the baseball, and a specific way to throw the curveball to make it curve. In this little how to, I will teach you both how to grip the ball, and what way to throw the ball. I will also provide pictures and other sources to show how to as well. There are rules to how to throw a curveball:

  • First of all, it takes years of practice.
  • You have to grip the ball a certain way to force the ball to spin how you want it to spin.
  • The more the spin, the better the curve.

Throwing the Curveball

When you throw the curveball, you have to throw it with a clockwise flick(for lefties, it's counter-clockwise.) Another way is throwing it while your arm is twisted clockwise(or counter-clockwise) relative to you. Flicking it will give your better results. However, as a beginner, I encourage you to keep your arm twisted clockwise(or counter-clockwise, if you are a lefty thrower.)

  • Remember to keep your arm twisted when you follow through.
  • Also, do not be afraid to let go of it kind of late, you want it do break low.
  • Once you get better, you can add in the flick, the curveball will break even more.

How Does a Curveball Break and Curve?

Believe it or not, there is a lot of physics behind the curveball. I'm not much of a scientist, but I can say from what i have learned, that the flick from the throw, and the way the ball spins, causes the air around the ball to force it to break. I have seen an experiment done on a basketball that was spun, and the ball curved up and started flying, of course going down still.
